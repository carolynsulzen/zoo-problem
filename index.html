Wild Animal Mini-Zoo

Animals at the mini-zoo!

This webpage is designed to help you learn more about the animals located here at the mini-zoo! Please reference below for the names of our residents as well as some additional information about the different species we look after.

Bears

  • Ollie
  • Mona

    • Ollie and Mona are both members of the brown bear species Ursus arctos horribilis, otherwise known as the Grizzly Bear! Historically, they have lived throughout North America, Europe, and Asia. For more information on Grizzly Bears please visit the link below.

    Fish and Wildlife Service

    Giraffes

  • Frankie
  • Coconut

    • Frankie and Coconut are both West African Giraffes, a subspecies of Giraffa camelopardalis. This species of giraffe is able to be differentiated from others due to their markings being a lighter tan color than other species. For more information on the West African Giraffe please visit the link below.

    African Wildlife Foundation

    Lions

  • Mella
  • Karl

    • Mella and Karl are both African Lions, or Panthera leo. Nicknamed the "King of the Jungle", these large felines are found throughout the African landscape. For more information of the African Lion please visit the link below.

    African Lion and Environmental Research Trust

    Monkeys

  • Cookie
  • Earl
  • Banana Pudding

    • The three monkeys that reside at the mini-zoo are all members of the Baboon species. Cookie, Earl, and Banana Pudding are all siblings born in the captivity of the zoo. Baboons are one of the world's largest monkeys and they can be found throughout the continent of Africa. For more information on Baboons please visit the link below.

    African Wildlife Foundation

    Alligators

  • Wren
  • Aspen
  • Mika

    • Wren, Aspen, and Mika are American Alligators residing here at the mini-zoo. These large reptiles are cold-blooded and need a warm environment to survive. They are typically found in hot swampy regions throughout the United States. For more information on the American Alligator please visit the link below.

    National Wildlife Federation
